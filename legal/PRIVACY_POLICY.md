## 2. What personal data we process

| Category | Examples | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Account data | Name, email address, role (admin / manager / user) | Created by your organisation's admin when your account is provisioned |
| Activity data | Item/kit checkouts and returns, reservations, condition notes, who performed each action and when | Generated by your use of the app |
| Audit log | A record of admin/management actions (e.g. role changes, item edits) tied to the user who performed them | Generated automatically by the app |

GearTrack does **not** currently collect or store photos, documents, or other uploaded files — that capability exists in the codebase but is disabled and unused.

We do not knowingly collect special category data (health, biometric, etc.) and the product is not designed to do so.

## 3. Why we process it, and our legal basis

We process this data to provide the equipment-tracking service your organisation has contracted us to provide: letting your organisation manage who has which item of equipment, when it's due back, and a history of that activity.

Our legal basis is **performance of a contract** (the contract between us and your organisation) and, for activity/audit logging, our and your organisation's **legitimate interests** in maintaining an accurate, accountable record of equipment custody.

## 4. Who we share it with

**Sub-processors:**

- **Google Cloud / Firebase** (Google Ireland Limited / Google LLC) — hosts our database (Cloud Firestore), authentication, and serverless functions. This is our only sub-processor.
  - Your account and activity data is stored in Cloud Firestore in the **europe-west2 (London)** region.
  - Account-creation requests pass briefly through Cloud Functions running in the **us-central1 (United States)** region before being written to the London database. This is a limited, transient international transfer, safeguarded by Google's Standard Contractual Clauses (incorporated into the [Google Cloud Data Processing Addendum](https://cloud.google.com/terms/data-processing-addendum)).
  - Firebase Authentication (sign-in/password management) is a global Google service not pinned to a single region.

We do not sell personal data, and we do not use any other third-party processors (no analytics, advertising, or email-delivery services are integrated into GearTrack).

**Other organisations on the platform:** GearTrack is multi-tenant — several customer organisations share the same underlying database — but application-level access controls (security rules tied to your organisation's ID) prevent any user from seeing another organisation's data. See the [DPA's security annex](./DPA.md) for detail.

**The platform operator:** [YOUR COMPANY NAME] retains a technical "platform admin" capability to access any organisation's data on the platform, used only for customer support, troubleshooting, and platform administration. This access is not used to view data for any other purpose, and is disclosed in full in the DPA.

## 5. How long we keep it

- **Account data** is kept for as long as your account is active, and is deleted when your organisation removes you or terminates its contract with us.
- **Checkout, reservation, and history records** are automatically purged once they are **older than 180 days**.
- **Audit log entries** are retained for as long as the related organisation's account is active (no automatic purge currently applied).
- Deleted data may still exist in encrypted daily backups for up to **7 days** after deletion, after which it is permanently removed (see Section 6).

## 6. How we protect it

- Every database query is scoped to your organisation's ID and enforced by server-side security rules — your organisation's data cannot be queried by another organisation's users, by rule, not just by application logic.
- Account creation is admin-invite-only; there is no open self-registration.
- Daily encrypted backups are taken automatically, retained for 7 days, enabling recovery from accidental data loss.
- No passwords are emailed in plaintext; new accounts are activated via a single-use, time-limited reset link shared directly by an administrator.

## 7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to: access the personal data we hold about you; have inaccurate data corrected; have your data erased in certain circumstances; restrict or object to processing; and receive your data in a portable format. Because your organisation is the data controller, please raise these requests with them in the first instance — we will support them in responding within the statutory time limits.

You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at [ico.org.uk](https://ico.org.uk) if you believe your data has been mishandled.
