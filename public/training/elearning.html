Welcome to GearTrack

This is a quick, click-through tour of how the team checks gear in and out, makes reservations, and manages the inventory. About 10 minutes, 11 short stops, a few quick checks along the way.

User
Browse inventory, create reservations, manage their own checkouts and history.
Manager (Team Member)
All of the above, plus add/edit items, approve reservations, and create/manage checkouts.
Admin
All of the above, plus Reports, the Activity Log, and the Admin Panel (users, backup).
Getting started

Getting an Account & Logging In

GearTrack doesn't have open sign-up. Every account is created by your organisation's Admin.

  • Ask your Admin to add you from the Admin Panel.
  • You'll get a one-time password-reset link — shared manually (email, Slack, whatever's easiest), not sent automatically.
  • Open the link, set your password, then sign in. You start as a User; an Admin can promote you later.
Home base

The Dashboard

First thing you see after logging in — a live snapshot of the inventory.

  • Summary cards — total, available, checked-out, and overdue items.
  • A banner up top if anything is flagged for inspection.
  • 7-day activity chart, upcoming reservations, and an active checkouts table.
Inventory

Items & Kits

Everything the team owns lives here, searchable and filterable.

  • Items — search by name, filter by category or condition. Click any item for its full detail page and audit trail.
  • Kits — named bundles (e.g. "Documentary Run & Gun Kit") that get reserved or checked out as one unit.
Booking ahead

Reservations

Anyone can reserve items or kits for a future date range — but what happens next depends on your role.

  • Go to Reservations → New Reservation, pick items or a kit, set a start/end date, submit.
  • If you're a User, it's created as Pending — a Manager or Admin has to approve it.
  • If you're a Manager or Admin, it's approved automatically — no extra step.

Handing out gear

Checkouts

Managers and Admins create checkouts from the Checkouts screen. There's an important quirk worth knowing here.

  • New Checkout — pick items and a due date, optionally add a condition report, confirm.
  • Quick Grab — a one-click checkout due back end of today, for fast loans.
  • The checkout always belongs to whoever creates it — there's no field to assign it to someone else. If gear is for a teammate, they should grab it themselves (in person, or via the mobile app).

Coming back

Returning Gear & Condition Flags

Checking gear back in always asks for a condition report — and a bad one has a real consequence.

  • From Checkouts, click Check In, rate the condition (Excellent / Good / Fair / Poor / Damaged), add notes.
  • Return it Poor or Damaged and the item is automatically flagged — blocked from booking until someone clears it.

On the go

My History & the Mobile App

Your own record of what you've borrowed, plus a lightweight mobile experience at /m.

  • My History — your own checkouts and reservations, past and present.
  • Mobile Browse — a list of currently available items. Tapping Quick Grab checks one out to yourself instantly, due end of today — there's no condition report or custom due date here, and no reservations either. For those, use the full site (it works fine on a phone browser too).
  • Mobile My Gear — what you currently have out, with a one-tap Return and condition report.
Manager+

Manager Features

Beyond what a User can do, Managers keep the catalogue and the record straight.

  • Managing items — add, edit, or delete items from the Items screen.
  • Flagging — manually flag an item for inspection from its detail page, independent of a bad return.
  • Activity Log — a full audit trail of checkouts, returns, reservation approvals, item changes, and more — who did what, when.
Admin only

Admin Features

Full run of the organisation: people, data safety, and the numbers behind it all.

  • Add user — the only way new accounts get created. Generates a one-time password-reset link to share manually.
  • Change role / Remove user — promote, demote, or remove teammates (you can't change your own role).
  • Backup & Restore — export items and kits to a JSON file; restore from it if needed. Doesn't cover checkouts, reservations, the log, or users.
  • Reports — usage per item/user, late-return rate, unused items, approval rates.
